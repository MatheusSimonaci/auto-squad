## Applying Platform-Native Copy (TikTok)

TikTok attention window: 2 seconds. You don't explain. You reveal. The Bible is doing the talking — we're just setting up the camera. The verse has to land with zero interpretation. The viewer hears the text and connects the dots themselves. That's platform-native for a biblical content channel.

---

## Verse Selection

**Selected Verse:** 1 Timóteo 6:10

**Full text (pt-BR — NVI):**
> "Porque o amor ao dinheiro é a raiz de todo mal. E, almejando o dinheiro, alguns se desviaram da fé e se atormentaram com muitas dores."

**Reference:** 1 Tm 6:10

**Why this verse:**
- Directly answers the hook about the most misquoted verse in Brazilian Christian culture
- The verse itself IS the correction — no commentary needed
- Specific enough to illustrate literally: "love of money" has a concrete visual (greedy hands, obsession)
- "se atormentaram com muitas dores" — emotional weight that resonates with Brazilians who've seen this dynamic
- The verse doesn't condemn having money — it condemns the obsessive love for it; that distinction is the entire video

**Verse selection criteria check:**
- ✅ Directly answers the hook (corrects "dinheiro" → "amor ao dinheiro")
- ✅ Specific enough to be illustrated literally in 5 seconds
- ✅ Emotionally resonant (alívio, clareza — not condemnation)
- ✅ No metaphorical stretching required

---

## Literal Illustration Description (for Seg 3 — ILLUSTRATION)

**What does 1 Tm 6:10 LITERALLY describe?**

The verse describes a person driven by love of money — not someone who possesses money, but someone whose hands and heart are consumed by the pursuit of it. "Almejando o dinheiro" = hands reaching, gripping, counting. "Se atormentaram" = face of suffering, not peace.

**Concrete literal visual for video_prompt:**
- Mãos crispadas segurando notas/moedas com expressão de obsessão e angústia
- Contraste implícito: o problema não é ter dinheiro, mas o que o amor por ele faz com quem o persegue
- Ambiente escuro, foco nas mãos e no rosto — estilo bíblico ilustrado

**Anti-pattern (BCD_002):**
- ❌ Generic "dinheiro é ruim" imagery (notas rasgadas, proibido)
- ❌ Abstract wealth symbols (bolsa lotada, carro de luxo)
- ✅ Literal illustration of "AMOR ao dinheiro" — the obsession, not the possession

## Tone Guidelines

**3-5 tone adjectives for this verse + theme:**
1. **Revelador** — corrige sem julgar
2. **Acolhedor** — não condena quem tem dinheiro; liberta a culpa mal direcionada
3. **Direto** — o versículo fala, não explica
4. **Sóbrio** — peso real das palavras ("se atormentaram com muitas dores")
5. **Íntimo** — fala para o cristão que carrega confusão há anos

**Tone traps to avoid:**
- ❌ Moralista/pregativo — o versículo não é punição
- ❌ Sensacionalista — não é "segredo" revelado, é texto bíblico lido corretamente
- ❌ Teologia denominacional — 1 Tm 6:10 é transversal a todas denominações
- ❌ Parecer um sermão — 20 segundos, o versículo fala por si

## Authenticity Check

**Does this hook + verse combination feel genuine to Brazilian Christians?**

Yes. 1 Tm 6:10 is one of the most cited — and misquoted — verses in Brazilian Christian culture. The misquote "dinheiro é raiz de todo mal" is reproduced in WhatsApp groups, church sermons, and family conversations. Revealing the actual text feels like clarity, not content marketing.

**Is the angle devotional or content marketing?**
Devotional. The video doesn't sell a product or lifestyle — it corrects a cultural misunderstanding using the Bible as the only authority. The viewer leaves with: "I've been quoting this wrong, and now I know what it actually says."

**Adjustment needed?** None. The verse and hook are perfectly aligned.

---

## Applying Document Don't Create

The content exists — 1 Tm 6:10 has been misquoted for decades. We're not creating a narrative. We're documenting the correction. The "story" is the gap between what people think the Bible says and what it actually says. That gap is real. We're just pointing the camera at it.
